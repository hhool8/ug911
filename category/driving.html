Driving games are a genre of video games that simulate the experience of operating various types of vehicles, typically focusing on cars, trucks, or motorcycles. These games range from realistic racing simulators, like Gran Turismo or Forza, which emphasize accurate physics and vehicle dynamics, to arcade-style games, such as Mario Kart or Need for Speed, where the focus is on fun, fast-paced action with exaggerated mechanics and environments. Players navigate tracks or open-world environments, competing in races, completing time trials, or exploring freely. Driving games often offer a variety of vehicles, tracks, and customization options, and can emphasize speed, precision, or adventure, catering to both casual players and driving enthusiasts alike.
